# Unit 2: Networks of Exchange (1200-1450)

## Overview
This unit focuses on how trade networks facilitated the exchange of goods, ideas, technologies, and diseases across Afro-Eurasia. Key focus: How did networks of exchange transform societies?

---

## Key Concepts

### 2.1 The Silk Roads
- Overland trade routes connecting China to Mediterranean
- Luxury goods: silk, spices, precious metals
- **Caravanserais**: Rest stops that facilitated trade
- Ideas spread: religions, technologies, diseases

### 2.2 The Mongol Empire and Trade
- **Pax Mongolica** (Mongol Peace): Safe travel across empire
- Standardized weights, measures, and legal codes
- Religious tolerance encouraged exchange
- Postal system (Yam) facilitated communication

### 2.3 Indian Ocean Trade
- Monsoon winds determined sailing seasons
- **Dhows**: Lateen-sailed ships
- Trade goods: spices, textiles, porcelain, ivory
- Key ports: Calicut, Malacca, Mogadishu, Kilwa

### 2.4 Trans-Saharan Trade
- Camel caravans across desert
- Gold from West Africa → salt from Sahara
- Spread of Islam along routes
- Cities: Timbuktu, Gao, Djenne

### 2.5 Effects of Trade Networks
- **Diasporic communities**: Foreign merchants living abroad
- Cultural diffusion and syncretism
- Spread of diseases (Black Death)
- Economic interdependence

## Cause and Effect Relationships

### Expansion of Trade Networks
**Causes:**
- Mongol unification creating safety
- Technological innovations (compass, shipbuilding)
- Demand for luxury goods
- Spread of common religions (Islam) facilitating trust

**Effects:**
- Wealth accumulation in trading cities
- Cultural syncretism
- Spread of technologies across regions
- Biological exchange (crops, animals, diseases)

### Spread of the Black Death
**Causes:**
- Originated in Central Asia
- Mongol armies and trade spread it
- Rats on ships carried fleas with plague

**Effects:**
- Killed 30-60% of European population
- Labor shortages → end of serfdom in some areas
- Religious upheaval
- Economic restructuring

### Growth of Diasporic Communities
**Causes:**
- Need for trusted trading partners
- Religious networks (Islam, Buddhism)
- Economic opportunities abroad

**Effects:**
- Cultural exchange and syncretism
- Spread of religions
- New trading techniques (credit, banking)
- Architectural and artistic influences

## Memory Tricks

- **Trade networks**: "SIT down" - **S**ilk Roads, **I**ndian Ocean, **T**rans-Saharan
- **Silk Road goods**: "SSHP" - Silk, Spices, Horses, Paper/Porcelain
- **Indian Ocean relied on**: "MON-soon = MON-ey soon" (seasonal winds = seasonal wealth)
- **Black Death spread**: "Trade spreads more than GOODS - it spreads BADS (bacteria)"
