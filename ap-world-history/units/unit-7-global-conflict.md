# Unit 7: Global Conflict (1900-Present)

## Overview
This unit examines the causes and consequences of global conflicts in the 20th century, including both World Wars and their transformative effects on global politics and society. Key focus: How did global conflicts reshape the world?

---

## Key Concepts

### 7.1 Causes of World War I
- **MAIN** causes:
  - **M**ilitarism: Arms race, glorification of military
  - **A**lliances: Entangling treaties divided Europe
  - **I**mperialism: Competition for colonies
  - **N**ationalism: Ethnic tensions, especially Balkans
- Immediate cause: Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand (1914)

### 7.2 Conduct of WWI
- Trench warfare on Western Front
- New technologies: machine guns, poison gas, tanks, aircraft
- Total war: Entire economies mobilized
- Global scope: Colonies involved, fighting in Africa, Middle East, Asia

### 7.3 Effects of WWI
- ~17 million dead
- Empires collapse: Ottoman, Austro-Hungarian, Russian, German
- Treaty of Versailles: War guilt, reparations on Germany
- New nations created
- Russian Revolution (1917)
- Seeds of WWII planted

---

## The Interwar Period (1918-1939)

### Treaty of Versailles (1919)
- Germany blamed for war ("war guilt clause")
- Massive reparations
- Military restrictions
- Lost territory and colonies
- Created League of Nations (U.S. didn't join)

### Russian Revolution and Soviet Union
**1917 Revolutions:**
- February: Tsar Nicholas II abdicates
- October: Bolsheviks (Lenin) seize power

**Soviet Policies:**
- War Communism, then New Economic Policy (NEP)
- Stalin's Five-Year Plans (rapid industrialization)
- Collectivization → famine (~6 million dead in Ukraine)
- Great Purges (political terror)

### Rise of Fascism
| Country | Leader | Key Features |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| **Italy** | Benito Mussolini | First fascist state, nationalism, corporatism |
| **Germany** | Adolf Hitler | Nazism, racism, anti-Semitism, expansionism |
| **Japan** | Military leaders | Militarism, emperor worship, expansion in Asia |

### Great Depression (1929-1939)
- Stock market crash → global economic collapse
- Mass unemployment
- Led to protectionism, nationalism
- Helped fascists gain power

---

## World War II (1939-1945)

### Causes
- Failure of Treaty of Versailles
- Great Depression destabilized democracies
- Appeasement of Hitler (Munich Agreement)
- Nazi expansionism
- Japanese imperialism in Asia

### Key Events
| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1939 | Germany invades Poland; war begins in Europe |
| 1940 | Fall of France; Battle of Britain |
| 1941 | Germany invades USSR (Operation Barbarossa) |
| 1941 | Japan attacks Pearl Harbor; U.S. enters war |
| 1942-1943 | Stalingrad; turning point in Europe |
| 1944 | D-Day invasion (Normandy) |
| 1945 | Germany surrenders (May) |
| 1945 | Atomic bombs on Hiroshima & Nagasaki (Aug); Japan surrenders |

### The Holocaust
- Nazi genocide of Jews and others
- ~6 million Jews murdered
- Also Roma, disabled, LGBTQ+, political prisoners
- Concentration and death camps

### Effects of WWII
- ~70-85 million dead (deadliest conflict in history)
- Atomic age begins
- United Nations founded
- Decolonization accelerates
- Cold War begins
- United States and Soviet Union emerge as superpowers

---

## Total War Concept

### Characteristics
- Entire population mobilized
- Civilian targets (strategic bombing)
- Propaganda essential
- Economic production = military production
- Women enter workforce

### Impact on Home Fronts
- Rationing of food and supplies
- Women work in factories ("Rosie the Riveter")
- Propaganda shapes public opinion
- Government expands powers
- Racism: Japanese internment in U.S.

## Cause and Effect Relationships

### WWI → Russian Revolution
**Causes:**
- Massive casualties demoralized troops
- Economic strain on Russia
- Weak tsarist government
- Revolutionary movements had support

**Effects:**
- Bolshevik takeover
- Civil war (1918-1921)
- USSR founded
- Communism as alternative to capitalism

### Treaty of Versailles → WWII
**Causes:**
- Harsh terms humiliated Germany
- Economic hardship from reparations
- War guilt resentment
- Territorial losses created grievances

**Effects:**
- Hitler used resentment to gain power
- Nazi Germany remilitarized, expanded
- WWII

### Atomic Bombs → Cold War
**Causes:**
- U.S. developed and used nuclear weapons
- USSR didn't have them yet
- Demonstrated devastating power

**Effects:**
- Nuclear arms race
- Cold War tension
- Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD)
- Nuclear proliferation

## Comparisons

### WWI vs. WWII
| Feature | WWI | WWII |
|---------|-----|------|
| **Main cause** | Nationalism, alliances | Fascism, failed peace |
| **Warfare** | Trench warfare | Mobile warfare, air power |
| **Deadliest** | ~17 million | ~70-85 million |
| **Genocide** | Armenian Genocide | Holocaust |
| **Ending** | Treaty of Versailles | Occupation, UN |
| **Aftermath** | League of Nations | Cold War, UN |

### Totalitarian States
| Feature | Soviet Union | Nazi Germany | Fascist Italy |
|---------|--------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Ideology** | Communism | Nazism | Fascism |
| **Economy** | State-controlled | State-directed capitalism | Corporatism |
| **Goal** | Class revolution | Racial purity | National greatness |
| **Methods** | Purges, gulags | Holocaust, camps | Secret police, violence |

## Memory Tricks

- **WWI causes**: "MAIN" - **M**ilitarism, **A**lliances, **I**mperialism, **N**ationalism
- **WWI alliances**: "Triple E vs. Triple A" - Entente (Britain, France, Russia) vs. Alliance (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Italy*)
- **Fascism features**: "NARD" - **N**ationalism, **A**uthoritarian, **R**acism/militarism, **D**ictator
- **WWII timeline**: "39-41-45" - Starts 1939, Pearl Harbor 1941, Ends 1945
