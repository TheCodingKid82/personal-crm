# Unit 9: Globalization (1900-Present)

## Overview
This unit examines the increasing interconnection of the world through economic, technological, and cultural exchange. Key focus: How has globalization transformed human societies?

---

## Key Concepts

### 9.1 Advances in Technology
- **Transportation**: Jets, container ships
- **Communication**: Internet, cell phones, satellites
- **Medicine**: Vaccines, antibiotics, green revolution
- **Energy**: Oil dependence, nuclear power, renewables

### 9.2 Economic Globalization
- Free trade agreements
- Multinational corporations
- Global supply chains
- Financial integration
- Outsourcing and offshoring

### 9.3 Social and Cultural Changes
- Migration patterns
- Cultural diffusion (and resistance)
- Global popular culture

## Economic Globalization

### International Organizations
| Organization | Founded | Purpose |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| **United Nations (UN)** | 1945 | International cooperation, peace |
| **World Bank** | 1944 | Development loans, poverty reduction |
| **IMF** | 1944 | Financial stability, currency |
| **GATT/WTO** | 1947/1995 | Trade agreements, reduce tariffs |
| **European Union (EU)** | 1993 (earlier forms) | European integration |

### Free Trade Agreements
- **NAFTA** (1994): U.S., Canada, Mexico → USMCA (2020)
- **EU Single Market**: Free movement of goods, people
- **ASEAN**: Southeast Asian economic cooperation

### Multinational Corporations
- Operate across national borders
- Examples: Apple, Toyota, Nestlé, Samsung
- Can have economies larger than small countries
- Criticized for exploitation, tax avoidance

### Effects of Economic Globalization

**Positive:**
- Economic growth in developing countries
- Lower consumer prices
- Technology transfer
- Increased living standards

**Negative:**
- Job losses in developed countries
- "Race to the bottom" in wages, regulations
- Economic inequality
- Environmental damage
- Cultural homogenization

---

## Social Changes

### Migration
- ~280 million international migrants (2020)
- Push factors: Poverty, war, persecution, climate
- Pull factors: Jobs, education, family reunification
- Guest workers, refugees, undocumented migration

### Urbanization
- 55% of world now urban (up from 30% in 1950)
- Megacities: Tokyo, Delhi, Shanghai, São Paulo
- Slums and informal settlements
- Strain on infrastructure

### Women's Rights Movements
- Expanding education for women globally
- Increased workforce participation
- Political representation growing
- Continued challenges: pay gap, violence, discrimination

### Human Rights
- Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948)
- International Criminal Court (2002)
- Human rights organizations (Amnesty, Human Rights Watch)
- Ongoing debates about cultural relativism

## Disease and Global Health

### Epidemics and Pandemics
| Disease | Period | Impact |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **Spanish Flu** | 1918-1919 | 50-100 million dead |
| **HIV/AIDS** | 1980s-present | ~40 million dead |
| **COVID-19** | 2019-present | Millions dead, global disruption |

### Medical Advances
- Vaccines eliminated smallpox, reduced polio
- Antibiotics (but rising resistance)
- Green Revolution increased food production
- Increased life expectancy globally

### Challenges
- Disease spreads faster with global travel
- Antibiotic resistance
- Healthcare inequality
- Pandemic preparedness

## Cause and Effect Relationships

### Technology → Globalization
**Causes:**
- Internet connects people instantly
- Container shipping reduces costs
- Jet travel shrinks distances
- Mobile phones reach everywhere

**Effects:**
- Global supply chains
- Cultural exchange
- Instant communication
- Information spread (and misinformation)

### Globalization → Inequality
**Causes:**
- Uneven benefits of trade
- Capital mobile, labor less so
- Automation replaces jobs
- Tax havens, corporate power

**Effects:**
- Growing wealth gap within and between countries
- Populist backlash
- Migration pressures
- Social instability

### Climate Change → Global Response
**Causes:**
- Industrial emissions (CO2)
- Deforestation
- Agriculture (methane)
- Global nature of problem requires global solution

**Effects:**
- International agreements (Kyoto, Paris)
- Green technology investment
- Climate migration
- Political conflict over responses

## The Big Picture: Continuity and Change

### What's New About Modern Globalization?
- **Speed**: Instant communication, rapid transport
- **Scale**: Truly global (not just regional networks)
- **Integration**: Interdependent economies
- **Technology**: Digital, not just physical exchange

### What's Continuous?
- Trade networks have always existed (Silk Road, Indian Ocean)
- Cultural exchange along trade routes
- Migration patterns follow economic opportunity
- Powerful entities shape trade rules (empires → corporations)
- Disease spreads along trade/travel routes
- Resistance to outside cultural influence
