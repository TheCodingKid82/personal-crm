# Unit 5: Revolutions (1750-1900)

## Overview
This unit examines the wave of political revolutions that transformed governance worldwide, driven by Enlightenment ideas, nationalism, and economic changes. Key focus: How did new ideas lead to political transformation?

---

## Key Concepts

### 5.1 The Enlightenment
- Reason over tradition
- Natural rights: life, liberty, property
- Social contract theory
- Popular sovereignty
- Separation of powers
- Religious tolerance

### 5.2 Causes of Revolutions
- Enlightenment ideas spread through print
- Economic inequality and taxation
- Weak or oppressive governments
- Influence of previous revolutions
- Rising middle class (bourgeoisie)

### 5.3 Effects of Revolutions
- Constitutions and written laws
- Expansion of voting rights (gradual)
- End of absolute monarchy in some places
- Rise of nationalism
- Mixed results for women and minorities

## Major Revolutions

### American Revolution (1775-1783)
**Causes:**
- "No taxation without representation"
- Enlightenment ideas (Locke)
- Distance from Britain, self-governance tradition

**Key Events:**
- 1776: Declaration of Independence
- 1783: Treaty of Paris (independence)
- 1787: Constitution written

**Effects:**
- First modern republic based on Enlightenment principles
- Inspired French Revolution
- Limited: slavery continued, women excluded

---

### French Revolution (1789-1799)
**Causes:**
- Financial crisis from wars (including American Revolution)
- Social inequality (Three Estates)
- Enlightenment ideas
- Weak leadership of Louis XVI
- Food shortages

**Key Events:**
- 1789: Storming of Bastille
- 1789: Declaration of Rights of Man
- 1793-1794: Reign of Terror
- 1799: Napoleon takes power

**Effects:**
- End of absolute monarchy
- Napoleonic Code (legal equality)
- Nationalism spreads across Europe
- Inspired revolutions worldwide

---

### Haitian Revolution (1791-1804)
**Causes:**
- Inspiration from French Revolution
- Brutal slave conditions
- Free people of color denied rights
- Enlightenment ideas

**Key Events:**
- 1791: Slave uprising begins
- 1793: France abolishes slavery in colonies
- 1804: Haiti declares independence

**Effects:**
- First successful slave revolution
- Only slave-led revolution to create a nation
- Ended French colonial ambitions in Americas
- Louisiana Purchase (Napoleon sells to U.S.)
- Inspired abolition movements

**Key Figure:** Toussaint Louverture - former slave, military leader

---

### Latin American Revolutions (1808-1830)
**Causes:**
- Napoleonic invasion of Spain/Portugal
- Creole resentment of peninsulares
- Enlightenment ideas
- Example of American/Haitian revolutions

**Key Figures:**
- **Simón Bolívar**: Liberated Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia
- **José de San Martín**: Liberated Argentina, Chile, Peru
- **Miguel Hidalgo**: Mexican independence movement
- **Pedro I**: Brazilian independence (peacefully)

**Effects:**
- Independent nations across Latin America
- Caudillo rule (military strongmen)
- Continued social inequality
- Land remained with elites

---

### Other Revolutionary Movements

#### Revolutions of 1848 (Europe)
- "Springtime of Nations"
- Nationalist uprisings across Europe
- Most failed but spread nationalist ideas

#### Taiping Rebellion (1850-1864)
- Largest civil war in history (~20-30 million dead)
- Led by Hong Xiuquan (claimed to be Jesus's brother)
- Weakened Qing Dynasty significantly

#### Meiji Restoration (1868)
- Japan's "revolution from above"
- Ended shogunate, restored emperor
- Rapid industrialization and modernization
- Avoided colonization

## Nationalism

### Definition
- Belief that people with shared culture/language/history should have their own nation-state

### Unification Movements
| Nation | Leaders | Method |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Germany** | Otto von Bismarck | "Blood and iron," wars of unification |
| **Italy** | Cavour, Garibaldi, Mazzini | Wars against Austria, popular uprisings |

### Independence Movements
- Greece (from Ottomans, 1821-1829)
- Belgium (from Netherlands, 1830)
- Many Latin American nations

### Effects of Nationalism
- Unification of Germany and Italy
- Disintegration of multi-ethnic empires (eventually)
- Ethnic conflicts
- Imperialism justified as national mission

## Cause and Effect Relationships

### Spread of Revolutionary Ideas
**Causes:**
- Printing press spreads Enlightenment texts
- Success of American Revolution
- Social and economic inequality
- Rising educated middle class

**Effects:**
- Wave of revolutions across Atlantic world
- Constitutional governments emerge
- Rights expanded (gradually)
- Conservative backlash

### Haitian Revolution's Global Impact
**Causes:**
- French Revolution's declaration of rights
- Brutal plantation slavery
- Large enslaved population majority

**Effects:**
- Terrified slaveholders worldwide
- Accelerated abolition debates
- Napoleon abandons America → Louisiana Purchase
- Proves enslaved people could win freedom

### Rise of Nationalism
**Causes:**
- French Revolution spread national identity
- Napoleonic conquests unified against France
- Romantic movement celebrated national cultures
- Print culture (newspapers, books) in vernacular

**Effects:**
- Unification of Germany and Italy
- Independence movements
- Competition between nations → imperialism
- Ethnic tensions in multi-ethnic empires
