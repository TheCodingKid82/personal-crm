# Unit 8: Cold War and Decolonization (1900-Present)

## Overview
This unit examines the ideological struggle between the U.S. and USSR and the concurrent wave of decolonization that reshaped the world map. Key focus: How did the Cold War and decolonization transform global politics?

---

## Key Concepts

### 8.1 The Cold War
- Ideological conflict: Capitalism vs. Communism
- No direct military confrontation between superpowers
- Proxy wars, arms race, space race
- 1947-1991 (Truman Doctrine to USSR collapse)

### 8.2 Decolonization
- European empires weakened by WWII
- Colonial peoples demanded independence
- Different paths: negotiation, violence, both
- Cold War superpowers competed for influence in new nations

### 8.3 Non-Aligned Movement
- Countries refusing to ally with either superpower
- Led by Nehru (India), Nasser (Egypt), Sukarno (Indonesia), Tito (Yugoslavia)
- Sought independent path, development aid from both sides

---

## The Cold War

### Origins
- **Ideological differences**: Capitalism vs. Communism
- **Post-WWII disagreements**: Division of Europe
- **Mutual distrust**: Soviet expansion, U.S. atomic monopoly

### Key Events

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1947 | Truman Doctrine | U.S. commits to containing communism |
| 1947 | Marshall Plan | U.S. economic aid rebuilds Western Europe |
| 1948-1949 | Berlin Blockade/Airlift | First major Cold War crisis |
| 1949 | NATO founded | Western military alliance |
| 1949 | USSR tests atomic bomb | Nuclear arms race begins |
| 1949 | Chinese Communist Revolution | Communism spreads in Asia |
| 1950-1953 | Korean War | First "hot" proxy war |
| 1955 | Warsaw Pact | Soviet response to NATO |
| 1956 | Hungarian Uprising | Soviet suppression of revolt |
| 1961 | Berlin Wall built | Symbol of divided Europe |
| 1962 | Cuban Missile Crisis | Closest to nuclear war |
| 1964-1973 | Vietnam War escalation | Major proxy war |
| 1979-1989 | Soviet-Afghan War | USSR's "Vietnam" |
| 1989 | Berlin Wall falls | Cold War ending |
| 1991 | USSR dissolves | Cold War ends |

### Cold War Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Containment** | Prevent spread of communism | Truman Doctrine, Korean War |
| **Deterrence** | Nuclear threat prevents war | MAD (Mutually Assured Destruction) |
| **Proxy wars** | Support allies in local conflicts | Vietnam, Korea, Afghanistan |
| **Propaganda** | Win "hearts and minds" | Radio Free Europe, Soviet media |
| **Space race** | Technological competition | Sputnik (1957), Moon landing (1969) |

## Decolonization by Region

### South Asia
**India and Pakistan (1947)**
- Gandhi's nonviolent resistance
- Partition: Hindu India, Muslim Pakistan
- Massive violence and displacement
- Conflict over Kashmir continues

### Southeast Asia
**Vietnam**
- French defeated at Dien Bien Phu (1954)
- Divided into North and South
- U.S. involvement escalates (1964-1973)
- Communist victory (1975)

**Indonesia**
- Sukarno declares independence (1945)
- Dutch resistance until 1949
- Non-Aligned Movement leader

### Middle East
**Israel/Palestine**
- Israel declared (1948)
- Arab-Israeli wars (1948, 1967, 1973)
- Ongoing conflict

**Egypt**
- Nasser nationalizes Suez Canal (1956)
- Suez Crisis: Britain/France/Israel intervene
- U.S. and USSR force withdrawal

**Iran**
- Mossadegh nationalized oil (1951)
- CIA coup restores Shah (1953)
- Islamic Revolution (1979)

### Africa
**Patterns:**
- Most gained independence 1960s
- Some peaceful transitions, some violent
- Cold War influence on new governments

**Key Examples:**
| Country | Independence | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Ghana | 1957 | First sub-Saharan colony freed; Kwame Nkrumah |
| Algeria | 1962 | Violent war against France |
| Congo | 1960 | Immediate civil war, Lumumba assassinated |
| Kenya | 1963 | Mau Mau uprising |
| Zimbabwe | 1980 | Long guerrilla war |
| South Africa | 1994 | End of apartheid; Mandela |

### Latin America (Cold War Context)
- Already independent, but Cold War battleground
- Cuban Revolution (1959): Castro allies with USSR
- U.S. interventions: Guatemala (1954), Chile (1973), Nicaragua (1980s)

## Methods of Independence

### Nonviolent Resistance
- India (Gandhi): Boycotts, civil disobedience, fasting
- Ghana (Nkrumah): Political organizing, strikes

### Armed Struggle
- Algeria: Brutal guerrilla war against France
- Vietnam: Defeated French, then U.S.
- Kenya: Mau Mau uprising

### Negotiated Independence
- Many African states in 1960s
- Britain withdrew from most colonies relatively peacefully

## Cause and Effect Relationships

### WWII → Decolonization
**Causes:**
- European powers weakened and in debt
- Colonial soldiers fought for freedom, expected it
- Atlantic Charter promised self-determination
- Cold War superpowers anti-colonial

**Effects:**
- Dozens of new nations by 1970
- Power vacuum, sometimes civil war
- Cold War competition in new nations

### Cold War → Proxy Wars
**Causes:**
- Superpowers couldn't fight directly (nuclear risk)
- Ideological competition
- Strategic locations (Korea, Vietnam, Afghanistan)

**Effects:**
- Millions died in proxy conflicts
- Local politics distorted by superpower support
- Arms proliferation
- Long-term instability

### Decolonization → New Challenges
**Causes:**
- Colonial borders ignored ethnic groups
- Colonial rule left limited infrastructure
- Cold War powers sought influence

**Effects:**
- Ethnic conflicts (Nigeria, Rwanda)
- Economic dependence (neocolonialism)
- Authoritarian governments
- Border disputes

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Cold War** | Ideological conflict between U.S. and USSR without direct war |
| **Containment** | U.S. policy to prevent spread of communism |
| **Proxy war** | Conflict where superpowers support opposing sides |
| **Decolonization** | Process by which colonies gain independence |
| **Non-Aligned Movement** | Countries refusing to align with either superpower |
| **Partition** | Division of territory (e.g., India/Pakistan) |
| **Apartheid** | South African system of racial segregation |
| **Neocolonialism** | Economic control without formal colonial rule |
