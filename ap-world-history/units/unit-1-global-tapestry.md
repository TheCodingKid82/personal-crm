# Unit 1: The Global Tapestry (1200-1450)

## Overview
This unit examines the diverse political, economic, and cultural developments across the world before major transoceanic contact. Key focus: How did states develop and maintain power?

---

## Key Concepts

### 1.1 Developments in East Asia
- **Song Dynasty China** (960-1279): Economic revolution, Neo-Confucianism
- **Mongol Empire** (1206-1368): Largest contiguous land empire
- **Yuan Dynasty** (1271-1368): Mongol rule in China
- **Ming Dynasty** (1368-1644): Restoration of Han Chinese rule
- **Japan**: Feudalism, samurai, shogunate system

### 1.2 Developments in Dar al-Islam
- **Abbasid Caliphate decline** and fragmentation
- **Delhi Sultanate** (1206-1526): Muslim rule in India
- **Mali Empire**: Trans-Saharan trade, Islamic scholarship
- **Islamic scholarship**: Preserved and advanced Greek/Roman knowledge

### 1.3 Developments in South and Southeast Asia
- **Hindu and Buddhist states** in Southeast Asia
- **Khmer Empire** (Cambodia): Angkor Wat
- **Srivijaya** and **Majapahit**: Maritime trade empires
- **Chola Dynasty**: Indian Ocean trade

### 1.4 State Building in the Americas
- **Aztec Empire** (Mexica): Tribute system, Tenochtitlan
- **Inca Empire**: Road system, mit'a labor, quipu
- **Maya city-states**: Continued development

### 1.5 State Building in Africa
- **Mali Empire**: Gold-salt trade, Mansa Musa
- **Great Zimbabwe**: Stone architecture, gold trade
- **Ethiopian Kingdoms**: Christian state, trade with Middle East
- **Swahili Coast city-states**: Indian Ocean trade

## Cause and Effect Relationships

### Mongol Conquests
**Causes:**
- Genghis Khan's military innovations (composite bow, cavalry tactics)
- Unification of nomadic tribes
- Desire for trade goods and tribute

**Effects:**
- Pax Mongolica: Safe trade routes across Eurasia
- Spread of technologies (gunpowder, printing)
- Spread of Black Death along trade routes
- Cultural exchange between East and West
- Collapse of some states (Abbasid, Song)

### Growth of Trans-Saharan Trade
**Causes:**
- Demand for gold in Mediterranean world
- Introduction of camel to Sahara
- Spread of Islam creating trade networks

**Effects:**
- Rise of West African empires (Ghana, Mali, Songhai)
- Spread of Islam in West Africa
- Growth of cities like Timbuktu
- Exchange of ideas and technologies

### Song Dynasty Economic Revolution
**Causes:**
- Agricultural innovations (Champa rice)
- Government investment in infrastructure
- Merit-based bureaucracy

**Effects:**
- Population growth (100 million+)
- First paper money
- Proto-industrialization
- Urban growth (Hangzhou largest city in world)
