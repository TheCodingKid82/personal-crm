# Key Terms Flashcards

Format: **Term** | Definition

Use for quick review. Quiz yourself by covering the right column.

---

## Unit 1-2: 1200-1450

**Pax Mongolica** | Period of peace and stability under Mongol rule that facilitated trade across Eurasia

**Silk Road** | Network of trade routes connecting China to the Mediterranean

**Caravanserai** | Roadside inn where travelers on trade routes could rest and resupply

**Dhow** | Lateen-sailed ship used for trade in the Indian Ocean

**Monsoon** | Seasonal wind patterns in the Indian Ocean that determined sailing times

**Diasporic community** | Group of people living outside their ancestral homeland while maintaining cultural connections

**Syncretism** | Blending of different religious or cultural beliefs and practices

**Neo-Confucianism** | Revival of Confucian thought in Song China, incorporating Buddhist and Daoist elements

**Mandate of Heaven** | Chinese belief that heaven grants emperors the right to rule based on their virtue

**Tribute system** | System where neighboring states acknowledged Chinese superiority with gifts

**Devshirme** | Ottoman practice of taking Christian boys to train as soldiers (Janissaries) or officials

**Millet system** | Ottoman practice of allowing religious communities self-governance

**Quipu** | Inca recording device using knotted strings

**Chinampas** | Aztec floating gardens for agriculture

**Mit'a** | Inca labor tax requiring service to the state

---

## Unit 3-4: 1450-1750

**Caravel** | Small, maneuverable ship with lateen sails used by Portuguese explorers

**Astrolabe** | Navigation instrument used to determine latitude

**Columbian Exchange** | Transfer of plants, animals, diseases, and people between hemispheres after 1492

**Encomienda** | Spanish system granting colonists the right to indigenous labor

**Hacienda** | Large agricultural estate in Spanish America

**Middle Passage** | The voyage of enslaved Africans across the Atlantic

**Mercantilism** | Economic policy that colonies exist to benefit the mother country

**Joint-stock company** | Business owned by shareholders who share profits and losses

**Casta system** | Racial hierarchy in Spanish colonial society

**Creole** | Person of European descent born in the Americas

**Peninsulare** | Person born in Spain or Portugal living in the colonies

**Mestizo** | Person of mixed European and indigenous ancestry

**Mulatto** | Person of mixed European and African ancestry

**Gunpowder empire** | Land-based empire using gunpowder weapons to expand (Ottoman, Safavid, Mughal)

**Janissary** | Elite Ottoman soldier, originally from devshirme system

**Absolutism** | Political system where the monarch has unlimited power

---

## Unit 5-6: 1750-1900

**Enlightenment** | Intellectual movement emphasizing reason, natural rights, and progress

**Natural rights** | Rights people are born with (life, liberty, property)

**Social contract** | Idea that government's power comes from the consent of the governed

**Popular sovereignty** | Belief that political power belongs to the people

**Nationalism** | Loyalty to one's nation based on shared culture, language, history

**Liberalism** | Ideology favoring individual rights, free markets, limited government

**Conservatism** | Ideology favoring tradition, order, and existing institutions

**Socialism** | Ideology advocating collective ownership and workers' rights

**Communism** | System with no private property; workers own means of production

**Proletariat** | Working class who sell their labor for wages

**Bourgeoisie** | Middle class; factory owners and professionals

**Industrial Revolution** | Shift from hand production to machine manufacturing

**Factory system** | System of manufacturing in centralized locations with machines

**Urbanization** | Movement of population from rural areas to cities

**Imperialism** | Policy of extending control over weaker nations

**Social Darwinism** | Applying "survival of the fittest" to human societies

**Sphere of influence** | Region where one power has economic dominance without formal rule

**Protectorate** | Territory with local rulers under imperial control

---

## Unit 7-9: 1900-Present

**Total war** | Warfare involving entire nation's resources and population

**Trench warfare** | Combat fought from protected ditches (characteristic of WWI)

**Fascism** | Authoritarian nationalism with dictatorial power

**Totalitarianism** | System where government controls all aspects of life

**Appeasement** | Giving in to aggressor's demands to avoid conflict

**Holocaust** | Nazi genocide killing six million Jews

**Cold War** | Ideological conflict between U.S. and USSR (1947-1991)

**Containment** | U.S. policy to prevent spread of communism

**Proxy war** | Conflict where superpowers support opposing sides

**Decolonization** | Process by which colonies gain independence

**Non-Aligned Movement** | Countries refusing to ally with either Cold War superpower

**Apartheid** | South African system of racial segregation

**Partition** | Division of territory, often along religious or ethnic lines

**Neocolonialism** | Economic control of former colonies without formal rule

**Globalization** | Increasing interconnection of world economies and cultures

**Multinational corporation** | Company operating in multiple countries

**Free trade** | Trade without government tariffs or restrictions

**Green Revolution** | Agricultural innovations increasing food production (1960s-70s)

**Pandemic** | Disease outbreak affecting multiple countries or continents

---

## Cross-Period Terms

**Continuity** | What stayed the same over time

**Change** | What was different before and after a period

**Causation** | The relationship between causes and effects

**Comparison** | Analyzing similarities and differences

**Periodization** | Dividing history into distinct periods

**Primary source** | Document or artifact created during the time being studied

**Secondary source** | Analysis of events by someone not present

**Thesis** | Clear argument or claim in response to a question

**Contextualization** | Placing historical events in broader context

**Corroboration** | Comparing sources to verify information
