# Key Figures Flashcards

Format: **Name** | Time Period | Significance

Focus on WHAT they did and WHY it matters historically.

---

## Unit 1-2: 1200-1450

**Genghis Khan** | 1162-1227 | United Mongol tribes; created largest contiguous land empire; military genius

**Kublai Khan** | 1215-1294 | Mongol ruler of China (Yuan Dynasty); welcomed Marco Polo; expanded empire

**Marco Polo** | 1254-1324 | Venetian merchant; traveled to China; wrote account that sparked European interest in Asia

**Ibn Battuta** | 1304-1369 | Moroccan scholar; traveled 75,000 miles across Dar al-Islam; documented Islamic world

**Mansa Musa** | r. 1312-1337 | Mali emperor; pilgrimage to Mecca displayed enormous wealth; spread Islam in West Africa

**Zheng He** | 1371-1433 | Ming admiral; led massive maritime expeditions to Southeast Asia, India, Africa

**Sundiata Keita** | c. 1217-1255 | Founded Mali Empire; legendary warrior-king

---

## Unit 3-4: 1450-1750

### Explorers & Conquistadors

**Christopher Columbus** | 1451-1506 | Reached Americas (1492); initiated Columbian Exchange; opened transatlantic contact

**Vasco da Gama** | 1460-1524 | Portuguese explorer; first European to reach India by sea (1498)

**Hernán Cortés** | 1485-1547 | Spanish conquistador; conquered Aztec Empire with indigenous allies

**Francisco Pizarro** | 1478-1541 | Spanish conquistador; conquered Inca Empire; captured and killed Atahualpa

**Ferdinand Magellan** | 1480-1521 | Portuguese explorer; led first circumnavigation of globe (died en route)

### Indigenous Leaders

**Moctezuma II** | 1466-1520 | Aztec emperor when Cortés arrived; killed during conquest

**Atahualpa** | 1502-1533 | Inca emperor; captured and executed by Pizarro despite paying ransom

### Ottoman Empire

**Mehmed II (the Conqueror)** | 1432-1481 | Ottoman sultan; conquered Constantinople (1453); ended Byzantine Empire

**Suleiman the Magnificent** | 1494-1566 | Ottoman sultan; golden age; legal reforms; expanded empire to peak

### Safavid Empire

**Shah Ismail I** | 1487-1524 | Founded Safavid Empire; made Shia Islam state religion of Persia

**Shah Abbas I** | 1571-1629 | Greatest Safavid ruler; Isfahan's golden age; military and economic reforms

### Mughal Empire

**Babur** | 1483-1530 | Founded Mughal Empire at Battle of Panipat (1526); descended from Timur and Genghis Khan

**Akbar the Great** | 1542-1605 | Mughal emperor; religious tolerance (Din-i-Ilahi); expanded and consolidated empire

**Aurangzeb** | 1618-1707 | Mughal emperor; ended religious tolerance; expanded but weakened empire

### Other Rulers

**Tokugawa Ieyasu** | 1543-1616 | Unified Japan; founded Tokugawa shogunate; began Edo period

**Peter the Great** | 1672-1725 | Russian tsar; Westernization reforms; built St. Petersburg; expanded Russia

**Kangxi Emperor** | 1654-1722 | Qing emperor; longest reign in Chinese history; patron of arts; expanded borders

### Religious Figures

**Martin Luther** | 1483-1546 | Started Protestant Reformation with 95 Theses (1517); challenged Catholic Church

### Others

**Bartolomé de las Casas** | 1484-1566 | Spanish priest; advocated for indigenous rights; documented abuses

---

## Unit 5: 1750-1900 (Revolutions)

### Enlightenment Thinkers

**John Locke** | 1632-1704 | Natural rights (life, liberty, property); consent of governed; influenced American Revolution

**Jean-Jacques Rousseau** | 1712-1778 | Social contract; popular sovereignty; "general will"; influenced French Revolution

**Montesquieu** | 1689-1755 | Separation of powers; checks and balances; influenced U.S. Constitution

**Voltaire** | 1694-1778 | Freedom of speech; religious tolerance; criticized Church and monarchy

**Adam Smith** | 1723-1790 | Free market capitalism; "invisible hand"; wrote Wealth of Nations

**Mary Wollstonecraft** | 1759-1797 | Women's rights pioneer; argued for female education and equality

### Revolutionary Leaders

**George Washington** | 1732-1799 | Led Continental Army; first U.S. president; model of republican leadership

**Thomas Jefferson** | 1743-1826 | Wrote Declaration of Independence; Enlightenment ideals

**Maximilien Robespierre** | 1758-1794 | French revolutionary; led Reign of Terror; eventually executed

**Napoleon Bonaparte** | 1769-1821 | French emperor; spread revolutionary ideals; Napoleonic Code; conquered Europe

**Toussaint Louverture** | 1743-1803 | Led Haitian Revolution; former slave became military and political leader

**Simón Bolívar** | 1783-1830 | "Liberator" of South America; freed Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia

**José de San Martín** | 1778-1850 | Liberated Argentina, Chile, Peru from Spanish rule

**Miguel Hidalgo** | 1753-1811 | Mexican priest; began Mexican independence movement (Grito de Dolores)

### Later Nationalists

**Giuseppe Mazzini** | 1805-1872 | Italian nationalist; inspired unification; Young Italy movement

**Giuseppe Garibaldi** | 1807-1882 | Military leader of Italian unification; "Red Shirts"

**Otto von Bismarck** | 1815-1898 | Unified Germany through "blood and iron"; Realpolitik

---

## Unit 6: 1750-1900 (Industrialization & Imperialism)

### Industrialists & Inventors

**James Watt** | 1736-1819 | Improved steam engine; key to Industrial Revolution

**Eli Whitney** | 1765-1825 | Invented cotton gin; increased demand for slave labor

### Political Economists

**Karl Marx** | 1818-1883 | Communist theory; Das Kapital; predicted proletarian revolution

**Friedrich Engels** | 1820-1895 | Co-authored Communist Manifesto with Marx; supported Marx financially

### Imperialists

**Cecil Rhodes** | 1853-1902 | British imperialist in Africa; Rhodesia named for him; diamond wealth

**King Leopold II** | 1835-1909 | Belgian king; brutal exploitation of Congo as personal colony

### Reformers

**Muhammad Ali** | 1769-1849 | Egyptian ruler; attempted modernization and industrialization

### Asian Leaders

**Commodore Matthew Perry** | 1794-1858 | U.S. naval officer; forced Japan to open to trade (1853)

**Emperor Meiji** | 1852-1912 | Japanese emperor during Meiji Restoration; rapid modernization

**Hong Xiuquan** | 1814-1864 | Led Taiping Rebellion; claimed to be Jesus's brother

---

## Unit 7-9: 1900-Present

### World War I Era

**Archduke Franz Ferdinand** | 1863-1914 | Austrian heir; assassination sparked WWI

**Gavrilo Princip** | 1894-1918 | Serbian nationalist; assassinated Franz Ferdinand

**Woodrow Wilson** | 1856-1924 | U.S. president; Fourteen Points; League of Nations

### Russian Revolution

**Vladimir Lenin** | 1870-1924 | Bolshevik leader; led October Revolution; founded Soviet Union

**Joseph Stalin** | 1878-1953 | Soviet dictator; Five-Year Plans; purges; led USSR in WWII

### Totalitarian Leaders

**Benito Mussolini** | 1883-1945 | Italian fascist dictator; first fascist state

**Adolf Hitler** | 1889-1945 | Nazi dictator; started WWII; Holocaust; extreme nationalism and racism

**Emperor Hirohito** | 1901-1989 | Japanese emperor during WWII; later symbolic role

### WWII Leaders

**Winston Churchill** | 1874-1965 | British PM; wartime leadership; opposed appeasement

**Franklin D. Roosevelt** | 1882-1945 | U.S. president; New Deal; WWII leadership

**Harry Truman** | 1884-1972 | U.S. president; atomic bombs; Truman Doctrine; containment

### Independence Leaders

**Mohandas Gandhi** | 1869-1948 | Indian independence leader; nonviolent resistance (satyagraha)

**Jawaharlal Nehru** | 1889-1964 | First Indian PM; Non-Aligned Movement leader

**Ho Chi Minh** | 1890-1969 | Vietnamese independence leader; fought French and Americans

**Kwame Nkrumah** | 1909-1972 | Led Ghana to independence (1957); Pan-Africanism advocate

**Gamal Abdel Nasser** | 1918-1970 | Egyptian leader; Suez Crisis; Arab nationalism

**Nelson Mandela** | 1918-2013 | Anti-apartheid leader; imprisoned 27 years; first Black South African president

**Jomo Kenyatta** | 1897-1978 | Kenyan independence leader and first president

### Cold War Era

**Nikita Khrushchev** | 1894-1971 | Soviet leader; de-Stalinization; Cuban Missile Crisis

**John F. Kennedy** | 1917-1963 | U.S. president; Cuban Missile Crisis; assassination

**Mao Zedong** | 1893-1976 | Chinese Communist leader; Cultural Revolution; Great Leap Forward

**Deng Xiaoping** | 1904-1997 | Chinese leader; market reforms; opened China economically

**Fidel Castro** | 1926-2016 | Cuban revolutionary; communist Cuba; Cold War flashpoint

**Mikhail Gorbachev** | 1931-2022 | Soviet leader; glasnost, perestroika; ended Cold War

### Recent Figures

**Malala Yousafzai** | 1997- | Pakistani activist; girls' education; Nobel Peace Prize

**Greta Thunberg** | 2003- | Swedish activist; youth climate movement
